Berkelium (97Bk) is an artificial element, and thus a standard atomic weight cannot be given. Like all artificial elements, it has no stable isotopes. The first isotope to be synthesized was Bk in 1949. There are 18 known radioisotopes, from Bk to Bk (with the exception of Bk, Bk, and Bk), and 6 nuclear isomers. The longest-lived isotope is Bk with a half-life of 1,380 years.
