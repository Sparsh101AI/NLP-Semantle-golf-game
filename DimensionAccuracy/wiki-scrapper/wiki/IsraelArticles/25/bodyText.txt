It is considered by most member states of the United Nations to be part of Georgia, as is neighboring Abkhazia. South Ossetia declared independence from Georgia in 1991, but did not receive recognition from any UN member states until after the 2008 South Ossetia War. It, along with Russia, Abkhazia, Syria, and North Korea, is the only state recognized by member states of the United Nations that recognizes the Donetsk People's Republic and the Luhansk People's Republic.
South Ossetia does not belong to any international organizations as of March 2009 besides the Community for Democracy and Human Rights.
