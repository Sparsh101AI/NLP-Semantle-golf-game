Wampar (Dzob Wampar) is an Austronesian language of Wampar Rural LLG, Morobe Province, Papua New Guinea.
It is spoken in the 8 villages (wards) of Dzifasin (6°35′18″S 146°34′46″E﻿ / ﻿6.588454°S 146.579374°E﻿ / -6.588454; 146.579374﻿ (Chivasing)), Tararan (6°32′36″S 146°33′12″E﻿ / ﻿6.543318°S 146.553226°E﻿ / -6.543318; 146.553226﻿ (Tararan)), Gabsongkeg (6°35′04″S 146°45′25″E﻿ / ﻿6.584546°S 146.757023°E﻿ / -6.584546; 146.757023﻿ (Gapsongkeg)), Ngasowapum (6°34′30″S 146°49′22″E﻿ / ﻿6.575056°S 146.822698°E﻿ / -6.575056; 146.822698﻿ (Nasuapum)), Munun (6°34′24″S 146°51′00″E﻿ / ﻿6.573319°S 146.849903°E﻿ / -6.573319; 146.849903﻿ (Munum)), Mare (6°39′30″S 146°40′56″E﻿ / ﻿6.658412°S 146.682269°E﻿ / -6.658412; 146.682269﻿ (Mare)), Gabandzidz (6°43′27″S 146°46′12″E﻿ / ﻿6.724186°S 146.769987°E﻿ / -6.724186; 146.769987﻿ (Gabensis)), and Wamped (6°44′28″S 146°40′12″E﻿ / ﻿6.741034°S 146.670007°E﻿ / -6.741034; 146.670007﻿ (Wampit)).
